Pathfinder

Pathfinder's mission is to help families of neurodivergent children with continuous, compassionate support from the first concern through diagnosis and beyond. We combine empathetic, human-like AI with gold-standard coaching insights so that no parent feels alone and every child's unique strengths are nurtured and celebrated to help them build agency, resilience and happiness.

The problem

The neurodevelopmental pathway is characterised by delay, disconnection, and unmet need. More than 400,000 children in England are waiting for assessment, while nearly one million are already in contact with services in the UK and at least 10 million in the US. Families report feeling unheard and not equipped to support their children.

Children need

  • • Safe, supportive ways to be understood while waiting for or living with a diagnosis.
  • • Consistency between school and home.
  • • Strategies that help with daily challenges like homework, transitions, and emotional regulation.

Parents need

  • • Trusted, empathetic guidance rather than impersonal forms or static apps.
  • • Continuity of support: a tool that remembers their child's story.
  • • Practical, personalised strategies that make daily life more manageable.
  • • Confidence and wellbeing support for themselves.

Teachers need

  • • Simple, quick ways to contribute observations without excessive paperwork.
  • • Practical classroom strategies they can apply immediately.
  • • Evidence to support SEN and EHCP / IEP processes.

The solution

Pathfinder is an empathetic, AI-enabled coach for families of neurodivergent children. This means families feeling heard and being supported with personalised strategies. Pathfinder simplifies complex journeys by bringing together parents, schools, and clinicians into one trusted platform.

Pathfinder converses in a warm, understanding tone, uses reflective listening and adjusts its approach to match the needs of parents whether they need a quick tip or an in depth coaching session.

Pathfinder remembers prior interactions and refers back to past conversations when the parent returns

It collects structured details, stores them securely with parent consent, and explicitly requests consent while meeting GDPR standards and providing escalation pathways for safeguarding concerns

The platform is initially designed to handle ADHD, expanding rapidly to autism, ODD and other conditions.

Roadmap

Pathfinder evolves in three clear stages from an ADHD wellness coach for parents to a platform covering a broader range of conditions and then to a regulated medical device that links parents to teachers and clinicians. At each stage, the company will develop its own proprietary data sets and algorithms.

Y1

Pathfinder Coach

Direct-to-consumer wellness tool providing practical, personalised coaching while building a living family profile.

Y2

Pathfinder Platform

Expands across conditions, brings schools into ecosystem creating continuity between home and classroom.

Y3

Pathfinder System

Regulated medical device (UKCA Class IIa) embedded into NHS workflows as multi-condition diagnostic and support system.

Competitive Moats

The business will develop a number of competitive moats. Some will be in operation from the start and others will develop over time.

Proprietary agent design that makes parents feel heard and empowered. It meets them where they are, learns about the specific situation of their family and uses empathetic coaching tailored for their mood and availability.

Stickiness of parents having shared information about the child and family over a period of time.

Proprietary data model for risk factors, phenotypes and co-morbidities.

Integration of interfaces with parents, schools, children, clinicians and other professionals.

Flywheel from collection of behavioural data from users to continuously improve delivery of personalised strategies.

SWOT

Strengths Weaknesses
  1. Unique approach to solving the problem with complete focus on developing empathetic AI tools
  2. Access to high level expert resources including senior university faculty to develop proprietary in-house IP
  3. Speed to market of private company vs. universities and NHS working on the same problem who are slowed down by bureaucracy
  1. Early‑stage funding dependence – this round will not provide enough capital on its own to execute the business plan
  2. Small initial team – At the moment, only one full-time employee. The company needs to build out a qualified team
  3. Incomplete technology – We only have a prototype at the moment and the technology is unproven
  4. Lack of market validation – Estimates of willingness to pay are not yet validated by customer research or paying customers
  5. High customer acquisition costs – Other players have faced CAC of £100 or more. We have not yet demonstrated that we can do it for less.
